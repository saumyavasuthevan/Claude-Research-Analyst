## Executive Summary

Singapore's health and wellness market is on a high-growth trajectory — valued at USD 18.8 billion in 2025 and projected to reach USD 42.5 billion by 2034 (CAGR 9.5%). The country's Healthier SG national strategy is structurally reshaping preventive healthcare, with the Health Promotion Board (HPB) at the centre as the designated national health promotion authority.

Three forces define the current landscape: (1) a rapidly ageing population driving chronic disease prevalence, (2) digital health and gamification emerging as scalable behaviour change tools, and (3) a national policy shift toward preventive care over downstream treatment. HPB is well-positioned at the intersection of all three, with a government mandate, digital infrastructure (Healthy 365), and a proven 3P partnership model. Key gaps include limited reach to elderly low-digital-literacy segments, mental health as an underdeveloped programme area, and the need to demonstrate clinical integration impact under Healthier SG.

## Market Segments

### Segment 1: Digital Health & Behaviour Change Platforms
- **What it is:** Mobile apps, wearables, and digital programmes for fitness tracking, nutrition, and behaviour change
- **HPB's position:** Healthy 365 + NSC — government-backed, reward-integrated, Singapore-specific
- **Market opportunity:** High — digital engagement is HPB's primary channel for reaching working-age adults
- **Key gap:** App UX and advanced tracking features lag commercial competitors (MyFitnessPal, Strava) [ASSUMPTION]

### Segment 2: Food Environment & Nutrition
- **What it is:** Nutrition labelling, healthier food supply incentives, foodservice reform
- **HPB's position:** Healthier Choice Symbol + Healthier Dining Programme — embedded across all food environments in Singapore
- **Market opportunity:** Medium — programme is mature; growth through deeper penetration of hawker/foodservice channels and sodium reduction initiatives
- **2024 focus:** HPB introduced two new sodium reduction initiatives [HPB Annual Report 2024]

### Segment 3: Smoking Cessation
- **What it is:** Public health programmes to reduce smoking prevalence
- **HPB's position:** iQuit programme + policy advocacy + public education
- **Market opportunity:** Declining segment (smoking rates already low in Singapore) but important for equity (lower-income groups have higher smoking rates) [ASSUMPTION]

### Segment 4: School & Youth Health
- **What it is:** Structured health assessment and education programmes for children and adolescents
- **HPB's position:** Health Plans for Primary 1–5 (piloting P4/P5 in 2025, full rollout 2026)
- **Market opportunity:** High — expanding school coverage builds lifelong health habits and creates early detection pathways

### Segment 5: Chronic Disease Prevention & Management
- **What it is:** Programmes targeting pre-diabetic, pre-hypertensive, and at-risk populations for early intervention
- **HPB's position:** DigiCoach pilot (2024–2025); health screening programmes
- **Market opportunity:** Very high — chronic disease is Singapore's largest long-term health cost driver; Healthier SG explicitly targets this
- **Key development:** DigiCoach AI coaching pilot (6,000 participants, July 2024–April 2025) is a flagship test of HPB's ability to scale personalised chronic disease prevention

### Segment 6: Workplace Health
- **What it is:** Employer-sponsored health promotion programmes for the working population
- **HPB's position:** Workplace wellness resources, NSC corporate challenges
- **Market opportunity:** Medium-high — corporate wellness spending is growing; HPB can deepen B2B partnerships with large employers

### Segment 7: Mental Health & Wellbeing
- **What it is:** Stress management, mental wellbeing, and psychological resilience programmes
- **HPB's position:** Limited — mental health is mentioned as a programme area but less developed vs. physical health [ASSUMPTION based on programme visibility]
- **Market opportunity:** High and growing — mental health awareness in Singapore has increased significantly post-COVID

## Competitive Analysis

### Healthier SG Partner Agencies (Collaborators, not competitors)

**Agency for Integrated Care (AIC)**
- Overview: Singapore government agency managing eldercare, Active Ageing Centres (AACs)
- Relevance: Overlaps with HPB on healthy ageing; both deliver under Healthier SG
- Positioning: Complementary — HPB is population-wide preventive health; AIC is eldercare-specific

**SportSG**
- Overview: Singapore's sports authority; manages sports facilities and national sports programmes
- Relevance: Physical activity overlap with HPB's NSC and active lifestyle programmes
- Positioning: Partners under Healthier SG; SportSG delivers facility access, HPB delivers programme engagement and incentives

**Communicable Disease Authority (CDA)**
- Overview: Singapore's public health authority for infectious disease
- Relevance: Overlaps with HPB on public health communication; non-overlapping in programme space
- Positioning: Complementary

### Private Digital Health Platforms (Displacement Risk)

**MyFitnessPal**
- Strengths: Largest nutrition and calorie tracking platform globally; strong user community
- Weaknesses: No government incentives; subscription-gated premium features; global, not Singapore-specific
- Market position: Used by health-motivated individuals; competes for Healthy 365 daily active users
- Pricing: Free tier + ~$19.99/month premium

**Strava**
- Strengths: Strong running and cycling community; social features and segment challenges
- Weaknesses: No government incentives; subscription model; limited nutrition and broader wellness features
- Market position: Active fitness enthusiasts; niche vs. HPB's mass-market reach
- Pricing: ~$11.99/month

**Apple Health / Samsung Health**
- Strengths: Pre-installed on devices; seamless integration with hardware; large user base
- Weaknesses: No Singapore-specific programmes; no government rewards; passive data collection without behaviour change programming
- Market position: Passive data aggregator; HPB integrates with these via Healthy 365

**Corporate wellness platforms (e.g., Wellbeing at Work, Virgin Pulse)**
- Strengths: Employer-funded; tailored to workforce needs; global reach
- Weaknesses: Not population-wide; employer-dependent; no government mandate
- Market position: B2B channel; potential HPB partners rather than competitors

## Market Opportunities

### 1. AI-Powered Personalised Chronic Disease Prevention
- **Market gap:** No national programme delivers AI-personalised health coaching at population scale in Singapore
- **Potential impact:** Significant reduction in diabetes and cardiovascular disease incidence if DigiCoach scales effectively
- **Requirements:** Positive DigiCoach pilot results; data governance framework for clinical+lifestyle data integration; vendor partnerships
- **Confidence:** High — DigiCoach pilot already underway; Healthier SG provides policy tailwind

### 2. Mental Health Programme Expansion
- **Market gap:** HPB's current portfolio is weighted heavily toward physical health and nutrition; mental health is underrepresented
- **Potential impact:** High — workplace stress, anxiety, and burnout are rising; MOH has signalled mental health as a priority
- **Requirements:** Clinical partnership with IMH (Institute of Mental Health) or private providers; evidence-based digital mental wellness tools
- **Confidence:** Medium — gap is clear but HPB's mandate and capabilities in this area are less established [ASSUMPTION]

### 3. Deeper GP/Clinical Integration under Healthier SG
- **Market gap:** Preventive programmes currently operate largely separate from GP clinical pathways; Healthier SG aims to bridge this
- **Potential impact:** HPB programme referrals through GPs would dramatically increase reach and programme credibility
- **Requirements:** Data-sharing frameworks; GP incentive alignment; MOH policy enablement (in progress under Healthier SG)
- **Confidence:** High — Healthier SG is explicitly designed to drive this; regional health clusters are building the infrastructure

### 4. Elderly and Low-Digital-Literacy Population Engagement
- **Market gap:** Healthy 365 and digital programmes disproportionately reach tech-comfortable working adults; elderly and lower-income Singaporeans are underserved
- **Potential impact:** High — these groups have higher chronic disease risk and lower baseline health
- **Requirements:** Physical access points (community centres, PCPs, Active Ageing Centres); simplified UX or non-digital programme pathways
- **Confidence:** Medium — HPB is aware of this gap; solutions require multi-agency collaboration

### 5. Corporate Wellness Deepening
- **Market gap:** HPB's workplace health programmes are currently toolkits and co-branded campaigns; potential for deeper co-funded employer partnerships
- **Potential impact:** Large Singapore employers (banks, tech companies, logistics) could fund joint wellness programmes with HPB branding and government rewards
- **Requirements:** Corporate partnership framework; co-funding model; programme evaluation reporting for employers
- **Confidence:** Medium — opportunity exists; requires commercial partnership development capability within HPB

## Technology Trends

- **Wearables mainstream adoption:** Fitness trackers and smartwatches now widely adopted in Singapore — HPB's wearable integration strategy is well-timed
- **AI coaching entering consumer health:** Noom, WeightWatchers Digital, and startup AI health coaches are validating the AI coaching model — DigiCoach is HPB's equivalent
- **Digital therapeutics (DTx) regulation emerging:** Singapore's HSA is developing regulatory pathways for software-as-a-medical-device — relevant to DigiCoach and future HPB digital products
- **Physiological sensing via smartphone camera:** Companies like Nervotec are enabling blood pressure and heart rate measurement via camera — could expand HPB's health screening capabilities without physical appointments [Syfe / Healthcare Innovation Singapore, 2025]
- **Interoperability:** National Electronic Health Record (NEHR) in Singapore could eventually enable seamless clinical + lifestyle data integration for HPB programmes [ASSUMPTION — consistent with Healthier SG direction]

## Recommendations

**1. Scale DigiCoach based on pilot evidence (High priority)**
- If April 2025 pilot results are positive, develop national rollout plan including funding model, vendor selection, and clinical integration with Healthier SG GP network
- This is HPB's biggest programme innovation in a decade

**2. Develop a structured mental health programme portfolio (High priority)**
- Partner with IMH, private mental health providers, and workplace EAP programmes
- Launch a dedicated Healthy 365 mental wellbeing module (mood tracking, stress management tools)

**3. Invest in Healthy 365 UX to close the gap with commercial apps (Medium priority)**
- Specific improvements: more exercise types for NSC, improved food database for nutrition tracking, social features
- Work with GovTech on iterative UX improvement sprints

**4. Build a formal corporate wellness partnership programme (Medium priority)**
- Structured co-funding model where large employers contribute to employee health challenges with HPB rewards top-ups
- Target: top 50 Singapore employers by employee count

**5. Expand non-digital engagement pathways for elderly and lower-income segments (Medium priority)**
- Partner with People's Association (PA) Community Centres and Active Ageing Centres
- Develop simplified NSC participation pathways that don't require smartphone ownership
