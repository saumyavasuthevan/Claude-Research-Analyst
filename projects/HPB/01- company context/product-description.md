# HPB Product Overview

> **Note:** HPB is a government statutory board. Its "products" are public health programmes, digital platforms, and policy instruments — not commercial SaaS. This document describes HPB's programme portfolio as its product suite.

---

## What is HPB?

The Health Promotion Board (HPB) is Singapore's national health promotion agency. Think **"national preventive health authority meets digital engagement platform"** — HPB designs and delivers population-wide behaviour change programmes, supported by the Healthy 365 mobile app, government-backed reward systems, and a network of public-private partnerships. Its mandate covers all Singaporeans across every life stage.

### Core Value Proposition

**For Singaporean residents and the Singapore government,** HPB is the national health promotion authority **that shifts population behaviour toward preventive health at scale.** Unlike private wellness apps (no government incentives) or clinical healthcare (treatment, not prevention), HPB **uses gamification, financial rewards, public-private partnerships, and evidence-based programmes to make healthy living accessible, affordable, and effortless for everyone.**

---

## Product Philosophy

### Key Principles

**1. Prevention over treatment**
- HPB operates upstream of the healthcare system
- Goal: reduce the burden of chronic disease and premature death by changing behaviour before illness occurs
- Aligned with MOH's philosophy of individual responsibility + affordable healthcare for all [hpfhub.info]

**2. Behaviour change through incentives and gamification**
- Healthy 365 uses Healthpoints, eVouchers, and challenge mechanics to sustain engagement
- National Steps Challenge uses wearable integration + prize draws + corporate competition
- Financial incentives (SimplyGo top-ups, FairPrice vouchers) create tangible motivation for health behaviour [GovTech, HPB]

**3. Environment design — make healthy the easy choice**
- Healthier Choice Symbol removes decision friction at point of purchase
- Healthier Dining Programme changes what F&B businesses offer, not just what consumers choose
- Embed health infrastructure into daily life environments (workplaces, schools, hawker centres)

**4. Data-driven and iterative — pilot before scaling**
- DigiCoach: 6,000-participant pilot before national rollout decision
- MOH and HPB integrate clinical + lifestyle data for targeted programme design [HPB Annual Report 2024]
- Programmes evaluated for population-level effectiveness (NSC studied in JAHA, 2022)

**5. 3P Partnership model — government cannot do it alone**
- People, Public, Private sectors each contribute
- F&B businesses, employers, wearable manufacturers, community organisations are all programme co-deliverers
- Funding assistance provided to food businesses for healthier menu innovation [NLB Singapore]

## Core Programmes / Features

### 1. Healthy 365 App

**What users can do:**
- Track daily steps via phone or connected wearable (Fitbit, Garmin, Apple Watch, Samsung)
- Log calorie intake and food diary
- Sign up for health challenges (National Steps Challenge, Eat Drink Shop Healthy Challenge)
- Earn Healthpoints for completing challenges and healthy actions
- Redeem Healthpoints for eVouchers (FairPrice, SimplyGo top-up, HPB Credit$)
- Access events listings and health programme information
- Connect physical activity data to chronic disease prevention coaching (DigiCoach, where enrolled)

**Technical details:**
- Available on iOS (App Store) and Android (Google Play)
- Developed in collaboration with GovTech Singapore [tech.gov.sg]
- Supports multiple wearable brands via Fitbit API and other fitness tracker integrations
- Caloric balance tracking: calories in (food logging) and calories out (physical activity) [GovInsider]
- Free to download and use — fully government-funded

**Differentiation:**
- **Government-tied rewards:** Only app in Singapore that delivers real monetary value (SimplyGo transport top-ups, supermarket vouchers) for health behaviour
- **National programme integration:** Single platform for all HPB challenges, unlike fragmented commercial apps
- **No cost to user:** No subscription or freemium gating

---

### 2. National Steps Challenge (NSC)

**What users can do:**
- Register via Healthy 365 app
- Track daily step counts using a wearable or phone
- Participate in individual and corporate team competitions
- Earn Healthpoints and enter prize draws based on step activity
- Engage in seasonal challenges with varying goals and incentives

**Programme details:**
- Launched in 2015 as a flagship nationwide physical activity programme [NLB Singapore, hpfhub.info]
- Expanded to include corporate teams in Season 2 [HPB press release]
- Targeted 250,000 participants in Season 2 [HPB press release]
- Uses wearables, smartphone app, and behaviour change techniques [JAHA study, 2022]
- Evaluated in a peer-reviewed study of 696,907 adults — published in Journal of the American Heart Association [JAHA, 2022]

**Differentiation:**
- Population-scale reach — hundreds of thousands of participants annually
- Behaviour change evidence base: academic study confirmed population-level physical activity impact
- Government-backed prizes create motivation not available to private competitors

---

### 3. Healthier Choice Symbol (HCS)

**What it does:**
- Front-of-pack nutrition label applied to food and beverage products meeting HPB's healthier nutrition criteria
- Criteria: lower in fat, sodium, sugar; higher in fibre and/or calcium (depending on product category)
- Enables consumers to quickly identify healthier options at grocery stores and foodservice outlets

**Programme details:**
- Run by HPB; food manufacturers and F&B businesses apply to use the symbol
- HPB provides funding assistance to F&B businesses for menu innovation to qualify [NLB Singapore]
- Covers grocery retail, hawker centres, restaurants, food courts, and schools

**Differentiation:**
- Government-certified label — higher trust than self-declared "healthy" claims
- Embedded in every food environment in Singapore — systematic environmental change, not just consumer education

---

### 4. Healthier Dining Programme

**What it does:**
- HPB partners with F&B outlets to promote and expand healthier menu offerings
- Funding assistance provided to food businesses to develop lower-fat, lower-sodium, higher-fibre options
- Hawker stalls, restaurants, food courts, and catering companies included

**Programme details:**
- Long-running programme embedded in Singapore's food culture [NLB Singapore]
- Stalls and outlets carrying qualifying dishes are promoted by HPB
- Works in conjunction with Healthier Choice Symbol labelling

---

### 5. DigiCoach (Pilot Programme, 2024–2025)

**What it does:**
- AI-powered digital health coaching for Singaporeans at risk of or with chronic conditions
- Personalised coaching, behavioural nudges, and progress tracking delivered via app
- Targets: chronic disease prevention and management (focus on pre-diabetes, hypertension [ASSUMPTION])

**Programme details:**
- Partnership with technology and healthcare companies [HPB newsroom, 2024]
- Recruitment started July 2024; pilot runs 10 months until April 2025
- Up to 6,000 Singaporeans enrolled in pilot
- HPB will evaluate effectiveness and feasibility before deciding on national scaling [HPB newsroom]

**Differentiation:**
- AI-powered personalisation — moves beyond one-size-fits-all campaigns
- Government-backed trust and data integration — cannot be replicated by commercial AI coaching apps
- Pilot-to-scale model — rigorous evaluation before national commitment

---

### 6. Smoking Control — iQuit Programme

**What it does:**
- Community-based smoking cessation support
- Builds a social network of support for smokers attempting to quit
- Covers: public education, peer support, fiscal policy advocacy, legislation, professional cessation support

**Programme details:**
- Launched 2011 as a ground-up, community approach beyond mass campaigns [NLB Singapore]
- Multi-channel: education, pharmacotherapy referrals, peer support groups, hotlines
- Fiscal and legislative dimension: HPB advocates for tobacco taxation and retail restrictions

---

### 7. School Health Programmes

**What it does:**
- Health Plans delivered to primary school students (assessments, health education, referrals)
- Covers: vision, hearing, dental, and general health assessments
- Structured health literacy and healthy habits education

**Programme details:**
- Currently covers Primary 1–3 students: 22,000+ students [MOH newsroom]
- Piloting expansion to Primary 4 and Primary 5 in 2025
- Full implementation across P1–P5 planned for 2026 [MOH newsroom]

---

### 8. Workplace Health Programmes

**What it does:**
- Programmes and resources for employers to implement worksite health promotion
- Includes physical activity challenges (NSC corporate), health screening, mental well-being resources
- HPB provides toolkits and co-branded campaigns for corporate partners
