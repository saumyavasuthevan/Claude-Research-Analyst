# HPB Competitive Landscape

> **Context note:** HPB is a government statutory board with a population-wide mandate — it has no direct commercial competitors. This document benchmarks HPB against:
> 1. **Peer national health promotion agencies** (global equivalents)
> 2. **Private digital wellness platforms** that operate in overlapping programme spaces in Singapore
>
> The framing shifts "competitors" to "peer agencies and displacement risks" — useful when evaluating HPB's differentiation, partnerships, or product gaps.

---

## Market Overview

**Market:** Singapore Preventive Health & Wellness
**Market Size:** USD 18.8 Billion (2025) [IMARC Group, 2025]
**Growth Rate:** 9.50% CAGR (2026–2034) [IMARC Group, 2025]
**Preventive Health Check-up Market CAGR:** 8.8% (2025–2030) [Grand View Research, 2025]

**Key Trends:**
- Growing emphasis on preventive care over treatment as Singapore's population ages [BTI Executive Search, 2025]
- Digital health and wearables increasingly central to behaviour change programmes at population scale [HPB NSC evaluation, JAHA, 2022]
- Healthier SG national strategy shifting Singapore's healthcare model toward primary and preventive care [PMC article, 2023]
- Chronic disease prevalence (diabetes, hypertension) driving demand for sustained digital coaching and community programmes [HPB DigiCoach announcement, 2024]
- AI and personalisation entering population health — from broadcast campaigns to targeted interventions [HPB Annual Report 2024]
- Public-private partnerships becoming standard model for health promotion delivery [hpfhub.info]

## Detailed Peer/Platform Analysis

### 1. Taiwan Health Promotion Administration (HPA)

**Overview:**
- Government agency under Taiwan's Ministry of Health and Welfare
- Closest structural peer to HPB — dedicated national health promotion mandate
- Programmes: cancer screening, maternal and child health, tobacco control, obesity prevention
- Target population: ~23 million Taiwan residents

**Target Audience:**
- All Taiwan residents across life stages
- Focus areas: chronic disease prevention, smoking cessation, maternal health

**Pricing / Funding:**
- Government funded [DATA UNAVAILABLE — exact budget not publicly available]

**Strengths:**
- **Integrated national screening programmes** — cancer screening coverage is high
- **Tobacco control** — strong legislative and public education track record
- **Maternal and child health** — well-developed programmes

**Weaknesses:**
- **Digital engagement** — less gamification and reward-based engagement than HPB [ASSUMPTION]
- **Private sector co-delivery** — less structured 3P model than HPB

**HPB's Differentiation vs Taiwan HPA:**
- **Healthpoints / rewards system:** HPB uniquely ties government-backed rewards to health behaviours — Taiwan HPA does not have an equivalent [ASSUMPTION]
- **Technology depth:** Healthy 365 + NSC is more mature as a digital health platform
- **3P Partnership model:** More explicit private sector engagement

**When HPB can learn from HPA:**
- Cancer screening programme design and population coverage
- Maternal and child health programme structure

### 2. UK NHS Health & Wellbeing (Better Health Campaign)

**Overview:**
- NHS-branded public health campaign across England
- Operates NHS Weight Loss Plan app, NHS Couch to 5K, One You programmes
- Funded by UK Department of Health and Social Care

**Target Audience:**
- England residents (especially adults with chronic conditions, overweight adults, smokers)

**Pricing / Funding:**
- Government funded via NHS / DHSC [DATA UNAVAILABLE — exact budget]

**Strengths:**
- **NHS brand trust** — extremely high credibility with UK population
- **Clinical integration** — directly linked to GP referrals and NHS services
- **Scale** — serves ~56 million England residents

**Weaknesses:**
- **Fragmented apps** — multiple separate apps (Couch to 5K, Weight Loss Plan, etc.) vs. HPB's single Healthy 365 platform [ASSUMPTION]
- **Reward system** — limited gamification or financial incentives compared to HPB
- **Bureaucracy** — slower to iterate vs. Singapore's leaner statutory board model [ASSUMPTION]

**HPB's Differentiation:**
- **Unified platform:** Healthy 365 consolidates challenges, step tracking, rewards, and events in one app
- **Financial incentives:** HPB eVouchers and Healthpoints provide tangible behaviour change levers not available via NHS apps
- **Speed of innovation:** DigiCoach AI pilot demonstrates faster experimentation cycle

**When HPB can learn from NHS:**
- Clinical pathway integration — linking preventive programmes directly to GP/primary care
- Brand credibility and public communication at scale

### 3. MyFitnessPal / Strava / Garmin Health (Private Digital Wellness Platforms)

**Overview:**
- Commercial apps for fitness tracking, nutrition logging, and exercise communities
- MyFitnessPal: ~200M registered users globally [ASSUMPTION — company claims]; nutrition and calorie focus
- Strava: ~100M users globally [ASSUMPTION]; running and cycling community
- Garmin: device + app ecosystem; step and activity tracking

**Target Audience:**
- Self-motivated fitness enthusiasts globally
- No specific Singapore focus; no population-level health mandate

**Pricing:**
- MyFitnessPal Premium: ~$19.99/month (source: MyFitnessPal website)
- Strava: ~$11.99/month (source: Strava website)
- Garmin Connect: Free with device

**Strengths:**
- **Large user communities** — social motivation and accountability features
- **Advanced tracking** — broader exercise type support than Healthy 365
- **Global reach** — available internationally

**Weaknesses:**
- **No government-backed incentives** — cannot offer eVouchers or nationally-recognised rewards
- **No population health mandate** — not designed to reduce national chronic disease burden
- **Commercially motivated** — subscription revenue model creates different design priorities
- **Not integrated with Singapore public health infrastructure**

**HPB's Differentiation:**
- **Government-tied rewards:** Healthpoints redeemable for real monetary value (SimplyGo, FairPrice vouchers) — not possible for private apps
- **National programme integration:** NSC, Eat Drink Shop Healthy Challenge tied to national health campaigns
- **No cost to user:** Healthy 365 is free and fully government-funded
- **Population-level data rights:** HPB can use aggregate population data for programme design — private apps cannot do this with the same public health mandate

**When private platforms are used instead of HPB:**
- Users seeking advanced workout tracking (running splits, cycling cadence)
- International users or expatriates not eligible for HPB rewards
- Users preferring social/community features over institutional programmes

### 4. Agency for Integrated Care (AIC) — Internal Overlap

**Overview:**
- Singapore government agency focused on care for the elderly and persons with disabilities
- Overlaps with HPB on healthy ageing and chronic disease management for seniors
- Partners with HPB under Healthier SG

**Target Audience:**
- Seniors, persons with disabilities, caregivers in Singapore

**Strengths:**
- **Deep senior care expertise** — HPB is a generalist, AIC is specialist for eldercare
- **Community care network** — manages Active Ageing Centres (AACs)

**HPB's Differentiation:**
- HPB handles population-wide preventive health across all life stages
- AIC handles post-diagnosis care and support networks
- The two are complementary, not competing — both operate under Healthier SG framework

## Strategic Positioning

### HPB's Market Position

**Where HPB operates:** "National preventive health authority delivering population-wide health promotion through digital engagement, programme delivery, and public-private partnerships"

**HPB's advantages:**
- **Government mandate and budget security:** 5-year block budget from MOH with performance accountability
- **Exclusive reward infrastructure:** Only entity that can tie health behaviours to government-backed monetary rewards (Healthpoints, SimplyGo eVouchers)
- **National data access:** Population-level health behaviour data for programme targeting — not possible for commercial platforms
- **3P network:** Established partnership model with F&B industry, employers, healthcare providers, and community groups
- **Integration with Healthier SG:** Embedded into Singapore's national healthcare transformation strategy — ensures long-term relevance and funding alignment

**HPB's constraints (honest assessment):**
- **Government pace:** Innovation cycles may be slower than commercial platforms [ASSUMPTION]
- **App UX vs private apps:** Healthy 365 likely trails commercial apps in UX sophistication [ASSUMPTION — based on government app norms]
- **Geographic scope:** Singapore-only mandate; no international leverage of proven programmes
- **Reach gaps:** Segments not actively engaged (elderly with low digital literacy, lower-income groups) remain harder to reach

## Competitive Trends to Watch

### 1. AI-Powered Personalised Health Coaching
- Commercial platforms (Noom, WeightWatchers Digital, startup AI health coaches) are scaling AI-driven behaviour change
- HPB's DigiCoach pilot is a direct response — critical to evaluate results and scale quickly
- **Implication:** If DigiCoach succeeds, HPB can defend its position as the most trusted and incentivised health platform in Singapore

### 2. Wearable and Device Ecosystem Fragmentation
- Apple Watch, Garmin, Fitbit, Samsung each have closed health ecosystems
- HPB's Healthy 365 already integrates multiple wearables — but needs to keep pace with new devices
- **Implication:** Continuous wearable API partnerships are a maintenance priority

### 3. Healthier SG as an Accelerator
- Healthier SG is shifting Singapore's entire healthcare model toward preventive care
- HPB is a central delivery partner — this is a major tailwind for programme scale and government investment
- **Implication:** Align all programmes with Healthier SG KPIs to secure ongoing budget and visibility

### 4. Corporate Wellness Market Growth
- Employers increasingly investing in employee wellness as a retention and productivity tool
- HPB's workplace health programmes sit in this space — potential to deepen B2B partnerships
- **Implication:** Opportunity to co-fund and co-deliver programmes with large Singapore employers (GovTech, DBS, etc.)

### 5. Mental Health as a Growth Programme Area
- Mental health is an increasingly prominent focus globally and in Singapore
- HPB's current programme portfolio is weighted toward physical health
- **Implication:** Expanding mental health programmes (stress management, mindfulness) is a strategic gap to fill [ASSUMPTION]

## Key Takeaways

**What HPB should learn from peers:**
- **From Taiwan HPA:** Stronger cancer screening and maternal health programme design
- **From NHS:** How to integrate preventive programmes directly into clinical GP pathways
- **From private platforms (MyFitnessPal, Strava):** UX design, social accountability features, and advanced exercise tracking

**What HPB should avoid:**
- **App fragmentation (see NHS):** Healthy 365 as a single unified platform is a strength — avoid splitting into separate niche apps
- **Purely broadcast approach:** 3P partnerships and gamification have already moved HPB beyond mass campaigns — maintain this direction

**HPB's sustainable advantages:**
- **Reward infrastructure:** Government-backed monetary incentives — impossible to replicate privately
- **Population mandate:** Unlike any commercial platform, HPB's mandate is population-level health improvement, which justifies data access and public funding
- **Healthier SG alignment:** Embedded in national healthcare transformation ensures long-term strategic relevance
