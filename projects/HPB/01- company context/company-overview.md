## Market Position

### Target Populations

**Primary:** All Singaporean residents, with focus on behaviour change across life stages
- Children and youth (school health programmes)
- Working adults (workplace health, National Steps Challenge, Healthy 365)
- Seniors (healthy ageing, chronic disease management)

**Secondary:** F&B businesses, employers, healthcare providers, community organisations
- Partners in delivering healthier food environments, workplace wellness, and community programmes

**Partner Ecosystem:**
- Ministry of Health (MOH) — policy and funding alignment
- Regional health clusters (Eastern, Central, Western Singapore)
- Agency for Integrated Care (AIC)
- SportSG
- National Parks Board
- People's Association (PA)
- GovTech Singapore (co-develops Healthy 365 app)
- Private tech and healthcare companies (e.g., Fitbit, DigiCoach partners)

### Market Size

**Singapore Health & Wellness Market:** USD 18.8 Billion (2025)
- Projected to reach USD 42.5 Billion by 2034 at CAGR of 9.50% [IMARC Group, 2025]

**Singapore Preventive Health Check-up Market:**
- CAGR of 8.8% from 2025–2030 [Grand View Research, 2025]

**HPB's role:** As the national statutory body, HPB operates across the full preventive health market. It is not commercially competing but sets the agenda and standards for health promotion in Singapore.

## Competitive Landscape (Benchmarking)

> **Note:** HPB does not have traditional commercial competitors. This section benchmarks HPB against peer national health promotion agencies globally and against private platforms that overlap in programme space.

**Peer National Agencies:**
- **Australia — AIHW / Health Promotion Australia:** Research-focused; less direct programme delivery
- **Taiwan — Health Promotion Administration (HPA):** Similar mandate; digital health integration comparable
- **UK — NHS Health & Wellbeing (formerly Public Health England):** Larger scale; NHS-integrated
- **Malaysia — Ministry of Health Malaysia (health promotion unit):** Less structured as standalone board

**Private Platforms Operating in HPB's Space:**
- **MyFitnessPal / Garmin Health / Strava:** Commercial wellness apps — no rewards tied to national programmes
- **Apple Health / Samsung Health:** Device-native; not incentivised through public health campaigns
- **Corporate wellness platforms (Wellbeing at Work, etc.):** Enterprise-focused; not population-wide

**HPB's Positioning:**
- Only national authority with government-backed incentives (Healthpoints, eVouchers, SimplyGo credits)
- Integrated with Singapore's broader Healthier SG national strategy
- Access to population-level data for targeted interventions — not possible for private platforms

## Product Philosophy

### Core Beliefs

**1. Population-level behaviour change over individual clinical care**
- HPB focuses on preventive health (upstream), not treatment (downstream)
- "Healthy living should be accessible, affordable, and effortless" [hpfhub.info]

**2. Technology and gamification as behaviour change levers**
- Healthy 365 app uses gamification, step challenges, and reward systems to sustain engagement
- DigiCoach programme uses AI-powered digital coaching for chronic disease prevention
- National Steps Challenge uses wearables + mobile app + rewards to drive physical activity at scale [JAHA study, 2022]

**3. 3P Partnership Model — no single-agency approach**
- HPB explicitly embeds private sector, community organisations, and public agencies
- Ground-up approach since 2011 [NLB Singapore]

**4. Data-driven, evidence-based programme design**
- Partners with MOH to use clinical and lifestyle data for targeted interventions [HPB Annual Report 2024]
- Pilots before scaling (DigiCoach: 6,000 participants pilot before national rollout)

## Key Terminology

**Healthy 365:** HPB's flagship mobile app for health challenges, step tracking, calorie logging, and rewards redemption
**Healthpoints:** In-app points currency earned through health activities; redeemable for vouchers and rewards
**National Steps Challenge (NSC):** Annual nationwide step-counting programme using wearables + Healthy 365 app
**Healthier Choice Symbol (HCS):** Front-of-pack labelling scheme identifying healthier food and beverage products
**Healthier Dining Programme:** HPB partnership programme with F&B businesses to promote and fund healthier menu options
**3P Partnership:** HPB's People-Public-Private sector collaboration model for health promotion delivery
**Healthier SG:** Singapore's national multi-year strategy (MOH-led, HPB-partnered) to shift focus to preventive healthcare
**DigiCoach:** AI-powered digital health coaching pilot programme for chronic disease prevention
**iQuit:** HPB's community-based smoking cessation programme
**Health Plans:** School-based health assessment and monitoring programme for primary school students

## Key Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Physical Activity Adoption (Mass Population)
**User:** Singapore resident, sedentary office worker
**Scenario:** Encouraged to be more physically active; joins National Steps Challenge via Healthy 365 app
**Before HPB:** No structured incentive to exercise; tracks steps passively if at all
**After HPB:** Downloads Healthy 365, joins NSC, tracks steps via wearable/phone, earns Healthpoints, redeems for eVouchers
**Value:** Population-level physical activity shift; 250,000+ participants per NSC season [HPB press release]

### Use Case 2: Healthier Eating at Point of Purchase
**User:** Singapore resident grocery shopping or dining out
**Scenario:** Wants to make healthier food choices but unsure how to identify them
**Before HPB:** No clear signal at point of purchase for healthier options
**After HPB:** Healthier Choice Symbol on packaging and menus makes identification instant
**Value:** Reduced friction for healthier food decisions; programme adopted by hundreds of F&B partners [NLB Singapore]

### Use Case 3: Chronic Disease Prevention via Digital Coaching
**User:** Singaporean at risk of chronic conditions (pre-diabetes, hypertension)
**Scenario:** Enrolled in DigiCoach pilot for 10-month AI-powered health coaching programme
**Before HPB:** Limited access to ongoing preventive coaching outside clinical settings
**After HPB:** Receives personalised digital coaching, behaviour nudges, and progress tracking via app
**Value:** Evaluated for chronic disease prevention effectiveness; 6,000-person pilot running July 2024–April 2025 [HPB newsroom, 2024]
